Audio Development

I was tasked with creating a new audio system, so I designed and implemented a dynamic audio system that is linked to the player's health bar. My main inspiration was Eternal Darkness: Sanity’s Requiem, which is a game that has a "sanity meter", and this meter can trigger certain events that distort the players perception of the world via audio and visual tricks. In the video below I used Staffordshire University's Mech Head framework for unreal as a way to trigger the audio code, so as you will see when the player steps on the land mine, they will lose 10 health, and the audio will become incrementally more distorted, to the point where even the peaceful background music at the beginning becomes uncomfortable to listen to.

(Headphone warning) The health is used to determine various things such as: the frequency in which disturbing sound effects play, the volume they play at, how much waveShape distortion to apply, at critically low health it plays a Flanger effect, which plays a delayed modulated copy of the sound making a eerie whooshing effect, and finally when health reaches 0 it plays a low sound effect. I had a lot of fun with this project, as it gave me the opportunity to be creative in a field of work I was previously unfamiliar on a technical level.
